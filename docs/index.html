What works — interventions with measured outcomes

Programs and policies that have moved the number — with comparison-group or longitudinal evidence behind them.

Girls Who Code — Summer Immersion
Participants +13.2 pp more likely to major in CS than a propensity-weighted comparison group. Alumni at 15× national average for CS majors (16× for Black/Latina alumni).
American Institutes for Research evaluation, March 2024 (ERIC ED647331)
BRAID Initiative — 15 CS departments
108% rise in women's CS enrollment over the BRAID period; women's enrollment growth outpaced overall growth at every participating institution.
UCLA / Linda Sax / NSF Award 1525737
CRA-W Grad Cohort for Women
67% of attendees intend to pursue PhD vs. 29% non-participants; 71% first-author publishing rate vs. 23% (1–3 years post-workshop).
PNAS / PMC5222789
Code2040 Fellows
~90% return-offer rate (vs. ~45–50% industry baseline); 100% subsequently work in tech. Intersectional by design — Black and Latinx CS undergrads.
Code2040 program data
Salesforce equal-pay audits
~$10.3M in cumulative adjustments by 2019; $5.6M in 2022 alone. Now publishes adjusted pay ratios by gender and race.
Salesforce equality updates, 2015–present
Colorado Equal Pay for Equal Work Act
State pay gap 78¢ (2021) → 86¢ (Jul 2025) — a 7-point gain in 3.5 years that does not show up at comparable speed in BLS national data.
Women's Foundation of Colorado / Tejara analysis · Cullen JEP 2024
AnitaB.org Top Companies benchmark
7 consecutive years of rising women's tech-hire share at participating firms; attrition reached near-parity with men. Program paused in 2024 — the only multi-year industry benchmark interrupted.
AnitaB.org Top Companies for Women Technologists 2023

What's missing — 17 evidence gaps logged

Where the data is silent, the policy is absent, or the program does not exist. These are the questions the literature has not answered yet.

  • Week 3No public dataset of AI-PhD completion rates by gender — the single largest gap in the evidence base. CRA tracks enrollment and degree production separately but does not publish cohort persistence.
  • Week 1No primary-source figure for women's share of Principal / Staff+ AI IC roles. The most recent senior-IC benchmarks (Facebook 15%, Google 10%) are from 2019.
  • Week 1No 2023+ figure for Black women's share of AI-specific technical roles. Most-cited statistic (3%) is from 2017 NCWIT and is not AI-specific.
  • Week 5No primary source isolates the AI engineer / ML scientist pay gap. BLS has no separate SOC code; Payscale and Levels.fyi do not publish AI-only cuts.
  • Week 5No intersectional (race × gender) pay data inside AI/ML roles — all available numbers are all-occupation.
  • Week 5No equity-equity audit at any AI lab. Given AI offers are 60–80% equity, this is the largest under-measured component of women's AI pay.
  • Week 4AI residency programs (Google, Meta, OpenAI, Anthropic) explicitly recruit underrepresented applicants but do not publicly disclose acceptance rates, gender composition, or conversion-to-FT rates.
  • Week 4No public AI hiring funnel (applied → interviewed → offered → accepted) split by gender.
  • Week 4No published 12-month retention rate specific to entry-level women in AI/ML. The 50%-leave-by-age-35 stat is all of tech, not AI.
  • Week 1, 4AnitaB.org Top Companies benchmark paused in 2024 — the only multi-year industry benchmark of women technologists at named employers is interrupted.
  • Week 2No standardized national data on women in AI-specific UG concentrations. IPEDS CIP exists but institutional reporting is too thin.
  • Week 2No national US attrition rate for women in CS undergrad. Single-institution figures (13.6% vs. 2.5%, 21% vs. 15%) are old or non-representative.
  • Week 2China CS undergrad gender data are not openly published at the national level.
  • Week 3No gender-disaggregated AI postdoc population. NSF GSS reports postdoc gender for all S&E but not AI as a subfield.
  • Week 32024 CRA Taulbee AI-specialty × gender cross-tab is not publicly summarized. Last published 2019 (women = 13.4% of AI/ML PhD recipients, 2012–2018 aggregate).
  • Week 3No measured longitudinal placement outcomes for WiML or Rising Stars in EECS. Both widely used; neither has published comparison-group data.
  • Week 5No India-specific AI/ML gender pay number from NASSCOM or Monster. NASSCOM 2024 covers AI/ML premiums but not by gender.
