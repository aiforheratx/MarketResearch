# Methodology

## Definitions used in this knowledge base

Because every report on women in AI uses a slightly different definition, the agent normalizes against these working definitions. Always state which one applies when reporting a number.

| Term | Definition we use | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Women in AI** | People who self-identify as women, working primarily on the research, engineering, productization, governance, or policy of artificial intelligence and machine learning systems. | Includes non-binary / gender-diverse people where the source includes them; flag when it does not. |
| **AI workforce** | Roles where >50% of the job is AI/ML-specific (research scientists, ML engineers, applied scientists, AI PMs, AI policy/ethics leads, AI founders). | Excludes generic "tech" or "data" roles unless the source's definition explicitly bundles them. |
| **Leadership** | Director-and-above in industry; tenure-track and above in academia; founder/CEO/CTO in startups; lead/principal in IC tracks. | When a source reports "senior" without defining, flag as ambiguous. |
| **Pipeline** | Education and entry path: undergraduate CS/AI, graduate ML, bootcamps, postdocs, conversion programs. | Pipeline ≠ workforce. Track separately. |

## The career-phase framework

Every "experience / struggle / need" finding gets bucketed into one of these phases. The dashboard tracks gaps and interventions per phase.

1. **Pre-entry** — high school, undergrad, considering AI
2. **Entry** — first AI role or first ML PhD year
3. **Early career** — years 1–5 in AI
4. **Mid-career** — years 6–12, including the "broken rung" to first management or first principal IC
5. **Senior** — director, principal, staff+, professor
6. **Executive / founder** — VP+, C-suite, founder, full professor, lab director
7. **Re-entry** — returnship, post-leave, post-pivot

## Statistic-handling rules

- **State the denominator.** "30% of [what total]?"
- **State the geography.** US-only, EU, global, OECD, etc.
- **State the year.** Always.
- **State the sample.** Survey of 1,200? Census of all NeurIPS authors?
- **State the definition.** What did *this source* mean by "women in AI"?

If any of those five are missing in the source, mark the number with a `[methodology gap]` tag in the report.

## The 4-W structure for every weekly report

Every report answers these four questions for its specific topic:

1. **What's the number?** — The current state, with citations.
2. **What's the trend?** — Direction and rate of change over 3–5 years if data exists.
3. **What's missing?** — Gap analysis: where the data is silent, where the policy is absent, where the program does not exist.
4. **What works?** — Documented interventions, programs, companies, or policies with measurable outcomes.

Followed by: **What to do.** — Recommended actions tied to the report's findings.

## Quality flags to apply

Use these tags in reports for transparency:

- `[primary]` — direct from primary source (the institution that produced the data)
- `[secondary]` — citing another report's citation of the data
- `[dated]` — statistic is more than 3 years old
- `[contested]` — at least one reputable source reports a materially different number
- `[methodology gap]` — denominator, year, sample, geography, or definition not fully specified
- `[paywalled]` — source behind a paywall; verify via abstract or quoted figure
