# Goals

## North star
Build, over 52 weeks, the most useful single-reader knowledge base on women in AI — one that answers, with sourced statistics, the questions:

1. **How many** women are in AI, and at what levels?
2. **Where** are they leading, and what roles do they actually hold?
3. **What career path** typically takes a woman from entry into AI to a senior or leadership role?
4. **At every career phase** — entry, early, mid, senior, executive — what are the documented struggles, the systemic blockers, and the unmet needs?
5. **What's missing** — in policy, education, hiring, promotion, funding, culture — that explains the gap?
6. **What works** — which interventions, programs, companies, and policies have measurably moved the needle?

## Weekly objective
Deliver one report that meaningfully advances the answer to one of the six questions above for one specific slice (career phase, geography, sub-field, role type, demographic intersection, or intervention).

## Success metrics (tracked in `dashboard/metrics.md`)
- **Reports delivered:** target 52/year
- **Unique primary sources cited:** target 200+ across the year
- **Distinct statistics logged:** target 500+
- **Time-series statistics tracked:** at least 10 (e.g., % women in AI PhD programs, % women on AI conference papers, women-founder AI startup funding share) tracked across the year so we can see movement
- **Action recommendations issued:** at least 3 per report, all grounded in that report's findings

## Anti-goals (what we are deliberately NOT doing)
- Not producing opinion content.
- Not generating "inspiring" profile pieces unless they sit inside a data-backed analysis.
- Not summarizing news. Reports synthesize evidence, not headlines.
- Not repeating the same statistics. A run that does not surface at least one new number or new source is a failed run.
